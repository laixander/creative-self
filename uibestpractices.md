I've removed the extra details (meta info, engagement stats, and tags) from the cards, leaving just the image, title, status, and description.

To answer your question: **Yes, this is definitely enough detail for the card view!** 

In modern UI design, this is actually a best practice known as "progressive disclosure." By keeping the cards clean and minimal:
1. **Reduces Cognitive Overload**: Users can scan the grid much faster without being overwhelmed by numbers and tags.
2. **Encourages Interaction**: It creates curiosity and gives a clear reason for the user to click the card to reveal the Slideover.
3. **Better Aesthetics**: The layout looks much cleaner, more premium, and less cluttered. 

The image and title are the most important elements for a user to identify a media post, so you've nailed the right balance here!
